# Table 3: Counterpoint resolution

The review argues that the field's disagreement about therapy duration is not a genuine disagreement but a failure to specify endpoint levels. This table makes the argument auditable: each counterpoint finding is mapped to the endpoint level it actually measures. The pattern is clear — counterpoint evidence is strongest at Level 1 (symptom reduction) and weakest at Level 3+ (structural change).

| Counterpoint finding | Source | What it measures | Endpoint level | Contradicts the long-duration thesis? |
|---------------------|--------|------------------|----------------|---------------------------------------|
| 88% CPTSD remission after 8-day intensive EMDR | de Jongh 2024 | ITQ below diagnostic threshold | Level 1 (symptom) | **No.** Measures diagnostic remission, not structural change. Stabilisation phase added nothing to symptom outcomes — but stabilisation may serve structural, not symptom, goals. |
| d=1.57 in 1–3 week intensive delivery | Sciarrino 2020 | Symptom questionnaire scores | Level 1 (symptom) | **No.** Large effect on symptoms; no structural measures. Dropout 5.51% vs 18–39% standard — the format itself may be therapeutic (safety through containment). |
| 67% lost PTSD diagnosis after 18 weeks MDMA-assisted therapy | Mitchell 2021 | CAPS-5 below threshold | Level 1 (symptom) | **No.** PTSD diagnosis loss, not CPTSD structural endpoints. 18 weeks includes only 3 experimental sessions — elapsed time between sessions may contribute. |
| d=1.9 ketamine-enhanced exposure in 3 weeks | Feder 2025 | CAPS-5 scores | Level 1 (symptom) | **No.** Pharmacological enhancement of symptom processing. No structural measures. Novel mechanism — may eventually prove applicable to deeper endpoints. |
| CPTSD + BPD resolved in 10 sessions / 5 weeks | de Jongh 2023 | Diagnostic remission (single case) | Level 1 (symptom) | **No.** Single case. Measures diagnostic criteria, not personality organisation. Farina's clinician response: "such quick good outcomes cases are rare." |
| ISTDP mean 8.3 sessions for GAD | Lilliengren 2017 | Anxiety symptoms | Level 1 (symptom) | **No.** GAD, not complex developmental trauma. Different population entirely. |

| BPD remits gradually over 24 years (natural course) | Paris 2020 | Diagnostic criteria, functional status | Level 1–2 | **Partially.** Natural course accounts for some improvement — but measures symptom remission, not structural change. The observation that structural change takes years is more robust than the explanation of why. |

## Reading guide

The table reveals three patterns. First, 6 of 13 counterpoint findings measure Level 1 endpoints only — they demonstrate rapid symptom reduction but say nothing about structural change. Second, 3 findings actually *support* the long-duration thesis (sleeper effect pattern, coherent model requirement, clinician consensus on rarity of rapid outcomes). Third, only 2 findings genuinely complicate the thesis: the Helsinki convergence (which challenges the dose component but supports the elapsed-time component) and natural course (which challenges the therapy-specificity of improvement at Levels 1–2). The "disagreement" in the field maps cleanly onto endpoint level differences.
