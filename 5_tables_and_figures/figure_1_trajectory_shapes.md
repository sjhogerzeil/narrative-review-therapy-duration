# Figure 1: Six trajectory shapes

Cross-layer analysis reveals six distinct trajectory shapes for healing from complex developmental trauma. Each is associated with different endpoint levels and populations. The trajectories are endpoint-specific, not modality-specific: the same patient may follow trajectory 5 for symptom reduction and trajectory 3 for structural change.

All panels share a common axis structure: horizontal = time (years), vertical = outcome/wellbeing. Dashed horizontal line = clinical threshold (symptom reduction). Treatment period indicated by shaded region; post-treatment period unshaded.

---

## Panel A: Dose-response

```
Outcome
  │        ___________________
  │       /
  │      /
  │     /
  │    /
  │   /
  │  /
  │_/
  └──────────────────────────── Time
   ├── treatment ──┤
```

**Shape:** Negatively accelerated curve — steep early gains, diminishing returns.
**Key data:** Shedler/Lambert: 50% improvement at 21 sessions, 75% at 40+, no ceiling at 2+ years [shedler-2020-tyranny-of-time]. Howard (1986): 50%/75%/85% at 8/26/52 sessions [howard-1986-dose-effect].
**Best fits:** Mild-to-moderate presentations; symptom-reduction endpoints (Level 1). The default assumption of the RCT literature.
**Population:** Patients with adequate pre-existing safety and shallow structural injury.

---

## Panel B: Sleeper / catalytic

```
Outcome
  │                        ___________
  │                      /
  │                    /
  │                  /
  │    ____________/
  │   /
  │  /
  │_/
  └──────────────────────────── Time
   ├── treatment ──┤  post-treatment
```

**Shape:** Modest gains during treatment, then continued or accelerating improvement after treatment ends.
**Key data:** Shedler (2010): effect sizes increase from 0.97 (end of treatment) to 1.51 (follow-up >9 months) [shedler-2010-efficacy]. TADS: non-significant at end of treatment, p=0.001 at 42-month follow-up [fonagy-2015-tads]. MBT: gains growing 5 years post-treatment [bateman-fonagy-2008-mbt-8yr]. Helsinki: PA patients improving most in years 5–7, after treatment ended [lindfors-2019-helsinki-10yr].
**Mechanism:** Kohut's borrowed-vs-owned distinction — therapy installs owned structure that continues developing through subsequent life experience. The reverse pattern (gains eroding after treatment) occurs when only borrowed structure was installed [abbass-2014-stpp-cochrane].
**Best fits:** Structural change endpoints (Level 3) in psychodynamic/psychoanalytic treatment.
**Population:** Patients who received sufficient dose to exceed the "transmuting internalisation threshold."

---

## Panel C: Spiral / recursive

```
Outcome
  │              *
  │          *       *         *  *
  │      *       *       *  *
  │  *       *       *
  │      *       *
  │  *       *
  │      *
  │  *
  └──────────────────────────── Time
   ├─────── treatment ────────┤
```

**Shape:** Oscillating upward trend — patients cycle through phases, returning to earlier work as deeper material emerges. Progress is real but nonlinear; setbacks are integral, not failures.
**Key data:** Herman (1992): three phases cycled repeatedly [herman-1992-trauma-recovery]. Blue Knot: "these phases are NOT linear — safety must be re-established repeatedly" [blueknot-trauma-practice]. Steele (2005): phase-oriented treatment with recursive cycling [steele-2005-phase-oriented].
**Best fits:** The dominant trajectory in the complex trauma treatment literature. Structural change + growth endpoints (Levels 3–4).
**Population:** Patients with moderate-to-deep structural disruption engaged in relational therapy.

---

## Panel D: Episodic / cyclical

```
Outcome
  │                              ___
  │              ___            /
  │             /   \__________/
  │    ___     /
  │   /   \___/
  │  /
  │_/
  └──────────────────────────── Time
   ├tx1┤  gap  ├tx2┤  gap  ├tx3┤
```

**Shape:** Alternating periods of intensive therapeutic engagement and self-managed maintenance, extending over years or decades.
**Key data:** Blue Knot: "continuously or episodically" [blueknot-trauma-practice]. myptsd-2021: cyclical engagement (intensive phases + self-managed periods) [myptsd-2021-how-long-therapy-2]. Bisson (2024): Davitt "on and off" for "many years" [bisson-2024-recognising-cptsd].
**Best fits:** Long-term trajectories where continuous engagement is not achievable or not needed. Growth endpoints (Level 4).
**Population:** Patients who consolidate between episodes; often driven by financial or access constraints.

---

## Panel E: Rapid / targeted

```
Outcome
  │    _________________________
  │   |
  │   |
  │   |
  │   |
  │   |
  │   |
  │___|
  └──────────────────────────── Time
   ├tx┤
```

**Shape:** Near-vertical step function — concentrated treatment produces large, rapid change.
**Key data:** de Jongh (2024): 88% CPTSD remission in 8 days [dejongh-2024-emdr-science]. Sciarrino (2020): d=1.57 in 1–3 weeks [sciarrino-2020-intensive-review]. Feder (2025): d=1.9 in 3 weeks [feder-2025-ketamine-exposure].
**Best fits:** Symptom reduction endpoints (Level 1). Often pharmacologically enhanced.
**Population:** Patients with high pre-existing adaptive capacity, adequate safety, and/or shallow structural injury. The safety construct suggests these patients have already partially cleared Factor 0.

---

## Panel F: Episodic / developmental

```
Outcome
  │                                    *  *
  │                                 *
  │                              *
  │              _______________*
  │             /               ├tx2┤
  │    ________/
  │   /
  │  /
  │_/
  └──────────────────────────── Time
   ├── tx1 ──┤  stable period  ├tx2┤
```

**Shape:** Distinct from cyclical (Panel D) because prior gains are retained across episodes. Each new therapy episode operates at a higher developmental level than the last.
**Key data:** Spring (2021): ~9 years therapy → ~3 years stable functioning → return after new trauma. "I knew what was happening. My trauma reactions made sense to me." Prior gains (self-compassion, psychoeducation) fully retained [spring-2021-falling-down-getting-back-up]. Heacock (2022): Sherlock demonstrated independent self-regulation during Erin's postpartum leave [heacock-2022-psychotherapy-twice-told].
**Mechanism:** The sleeper effect operating through post-therapy years consolidates owned structure that survives new crises. The second episode is qualitatively different — faster, more informed, less shame-laden.
**Best fits:** Multi-decade trajectories interrupted by new life events. Structural change + growth endpoints (Levels 3–4).
**Population:** Patients who achieved sufficient structural change in an earlier episode to retain gains across a gap.
