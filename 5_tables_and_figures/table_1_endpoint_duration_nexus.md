# Table 1: The endpoint-duration nexus

The review's central finding is that reported therapy duration is almost entirely determined by the definition of the endpoint. These five levels are nested, not parallel: each subsumes the previous ones. A patient who achieves structural change will necessarily also show symptom reduction and functional improvement. The levels and their timelines emerge from cross-layer triangulation of 89 sources.

| Level | Endpoint | Timeline | Evidence type | Key sources | Example measures |
|-------|----------|----------|---------------|-------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Symptom reduction** — loss of diagnosis, score below clinical threshold | Weeks to months | RCTs, intensive format studies | de Jongh 2024 (88% CPTSD remission, 8 days); Sciarrino 2020 (d=1.57, 1–3 weeks); Mitchell 2021 (67% PTSD loss, 18 weeks); Feder 2025 (d=1.9, 3 weeks) | CAPS-5, PCL-5, ITQ, BDI, CORE-OM below clinical threshold |
| 2 | **Functional improvement** — holding employment, maintaining relationships, reduced crises | Months to 1–2 years | Stepped care models, longitudinal studies | Links 2015 (GPM milestones: 6 wks → 6 mo → 12 mo → 18 mo); Fonagy 2015 (TADS functional gains at 42-month follow-up); Bateman & Fonagy 2008 (vocational improvement at 8-year follow-up) | Employment status, crisis frequency, GAF, social functioning scales |
| 3 | **Structural change** — personality reorganisation, mentalizing capacity, integration of dissociated parts, earned attachment security | Years | Long-term follow-up studies, case studies, naturalistic data | Lindfors 2019 (Helsinki 10-year: personality organisation still changing); Leichsenring 2013 (Perry: 50% PD recovery at 92 sessions, 75% at 216); Griffiths 2025 (DID: 3–5 years projected); Murphy 2009 (160 sessions / 5 years); Heacock 2022 (15 years weekly) | LPO, SCL-90, defence style, AAI classification, MID scores |
| 4 | **Growth and reconnection** — post-traumatic growth, identity transformation, capacity for intimacy and agency | Years to decades | Memoirs, practitioner essays, client narratives | Foo 2022 (10+ years); Walker 2013 (40+ years); Eger 2017 (healing ongoing at 90+); Joseph 2012 (PTG continuing "indefinitely"); reddit-2024-recovered-cptsd (5 years to "recovered") | PTG-I, narrative coherence, identity integration, relational capacity (no standard instrument) |
| 5 | **Recovery as direction** — "heading north"; functional markers of directional progress, no completion point | Ongoing | Memoirs | Spring 2020 ("heading north" metaphor: 15-year arc from DID to "undeniably good life") | Living mostly in "green zone," remembering without reliving, choosing from "front brain," experiencing joy alongside grief |

## Reading guide

Read downward for increasing depth: each level takes longer because it requires more fundamental change. Read the "Evidence type" column to see the systematic asymmetry: the shortest timelines have the most rigorous evidence (RCTs), while the longest timelines rely on observational and experiential sources. This is not because brief treatment is more effective — it is because it is more researchable. The "Example measures" column reveals the operationalisation gap: Levels 1–2 have validated instruments; Levels 4–5 have none. Duration claims at higher levels rest on clinical observation and lived experience rather than standardised measurement.
