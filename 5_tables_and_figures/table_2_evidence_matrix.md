## Reading guide

The table reveals three patterns. First, convergence is broad: 9 of 17 dimensions draw substantive contributions from all nine evidence layers, and no dimension draws from fewer than six. This cross-layer convergence strengthens the review's findings beyond what any single evidence tradition could support. Second, the strongest convergence zones are duration, trajectory, healing definitions, endpoint divergence, modality, phase structure, and adjuncts — seven dimensions with 9/9 layer coverage and density (●●● in most cells), meaning the findings rest on multiple independent sources within each layer. Third, the most consequential gaps cluster in two areas: the sleeper effect (6/9, absent from trauma-clinical, humanistic-existential, and neuroscience as direct evidence) and dropout (7/9, absent from neuroscience and memoir layers). The sleeper effect gap matters because this emergent construct — one of the review's strongest arguments for long-term therapy — rests primarily on naturalistic studies and counterpoint data rather than on the clinical traditions that advocate for extended treatment. The dropout gap matters because the survivorship bias critique rests on a narrower evidence base than the duration findings it qualifies.
