## Emerging pattern

**Strong consensus on phase structure, with an important caveat about execution:**

- The three-phase model (stabilisation, processing, integration) is endorsed across academic (Herman, Steele, Van der Hart), neuroscience (Porges, Perry, Lanius), and practitioner (Blue Knot 84% consensus, Alpern) layers.
- However, virtually every source that endorses phases also insists they are **nonlinear and recursive** — patients cycle between phases as new material emerges or life circumstances destabilise them.
- The psychoanalytic/relational tradition (Kohut, Bromberg, Benjamin, BCPSG) is **anti-phase** — describing organic, emergent processes driven by relational dynamics rather than treatment manuals.
- The EMDR counterpoint (de Jongh 2024, 2023) directly challenges Phase 1 (stabilisation) as unnecessary, but client experience (myptsd-2024) contradicts this for complex cases.
- Memoir evidence (Foo, Eger) shows phases visible in retrospect but not planned in advance.

**Resolution:** Phases may be useful as a MAP but not as a SCHEDULE. The clinical consensus is "phase-based in structure, organic in execution."
