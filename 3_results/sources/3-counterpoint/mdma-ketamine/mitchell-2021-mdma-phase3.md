## Summary

Landmark Phase 3 double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT testing MDMA-assisted therapy for severe PTSD. 90 participants (CAPS-5 ≥35) randomized to MDMA (n=46) or inactive placebo (n=44), each combined with manualized therapy including 3 preparatory sessions, 3 experimental sessions (8-hour sessions with MDMA/placebo, spaced 4 weeks apart), and 9 integration sessions. Over 18 weeks, 67% of the MDMA group no longer met PTSD criteria vs 32% placebo. Effect size on CAPS-5: d=0.91. Crucially for this review, 87% had developmental trauma history, and participants with the dissociative subtype of PTSD responded equally well. The study excluded current bipolar disorder, psychotic disorders, eating disorders with active purging, and dissociative identity disorder, but did NOT exclude personality disorders per se -- however, the exclusion criteria included "personality disorders with psychotic features" and the study was designed before BPD was systematically tracked. The FDA rejected MDMA for PTSD in August 2024, citing concerns about functional unblinding, therapist misconduct in earlier trials, and insufficient evidence of sustained benefit.

## Key claims
- MDMA + therapy: 67% no longer met PTSD criteria vs 32% placebo -- p. 1026
- Effect size on CAPS-5: d=0.91 (de jure) -- p. 1026
- Mean CAPS-5 change: -24.4 (MDMA) vs -13.9 (placebo) -- p. 1026
- Functional impairment (SDS) also significantly reduced: d=0.43 -- p. 1026
- Dissociative subtype responded equally well: MDMA delta = -30.8, non-dissociative = -23.6 -- p. 1026
- Equally effective with comorbidities including childhood trauma, substance use, dissociation -- p. 1026
- No increase in suicidality in MDMA group -- p. 1030
- 87% had developmental trauma; mean PTSD duration 14.8 years -- Table 1
- FDA rejected approval in August 2024

## Duration data
- Total treatment: 18 weeks -- Fig. 1
- 3 preparatory sessions, 3 experimental sessions (8-hour), 9 integration sessions = 15 therapy contacts total
- Experimental sessions spaced ~4 weeks apart

- No long-term follow-up in this trial

## Trajectory description

The trajectory is concentrated and pharmacologically enhanced. Three 8-hour experimental sessions form the core of treatment, each preceded by preparatory and followed by integration sessions. MDMA is hypothesized to create a "window of tolerance" in which patients can revisit traumatic experiences without becoming overwhelmed or dissociated. The therapy team (two therapists per participant) uses inner-directed exploration rather than structured exposure protocols. Change is rapid during the 18-week protocol, with continued improvement across the three experimental sessions. The design assumes that pharmacological enhancement can compress what would otherwise take much longer in conventional therapy.

## Relevance to thesis

**Endpoint divergence**: Primary outcome is CAPS-5 score change and loss of PTSD diagnosis. Secondary is functional impairment (SDS). This is symptom-reduction, not structural change. The absence of long-term follow-up means we cannot assess durability. The question of whether MDMA facilitates genuine structural change (as proponents claim via enhanced neuroplasticity and fear extinction) or temporary symptom relief is unresolved.

**Population match**: Partial but significant. 87% had developmental trauma, mean PTSD duration 14.8 years, high comorbidity. The dissociative subtype responded equally well. However, the exclusion of DID and the likely exclusion of current BPD (via screening procedures) means the most structurally disrupted patients were not represented. The exclusion criteria also removed people with active substance use disorders, psychotic features, and suicidal behavior requiring hospitalization -- precisely the complications common in severe complex trauma populations.

**Strongest version of the counterpoint**: MDMA may facilitate in 3 sessions what conventional therapy takes years to achieve, by pharmacologically creating the conditions for trauma processing. The finding that dissociative patients respond equally well is particularly important -- it suggests that the MDMA-induced state can bypass the dissociative barriers that conventional therapy struggles with. If MDMA reopens critical periods of neuroplasticity (as some researchers hypothesize), it represents a fundamentally different mechanism from relational therapy.

**Where it breaks down for our population**:
1. FDA rejected approval citing methodological concerns, functional unblinding, and therapist misconduct -- the evidence base is compromised.
2. No long-term follow-up -- we don't know if gains persist.
3. Excluded DID and likely excluded current BPD -- the most complex cases were not represented.
4. The "dissociative subtype" of PTSD (measured by CAPS-5 subtype score) is not the same as structural dissociation of the personality (as described by Van der Hart, Nijenhuis, Steele). The CAPS-5 dissociative subtype captures depersonalization/derealization, not the kind of part-based dissociation seen in DID-spectrum conditions.
5. MDMA is not FDA-approved and is currently unavailable outside research settings.
6. Ethical violations by therapists in earlier MAPS trials raise questions about the therapeutic frame.

**What the long-duration position can learn**: The MDMA model proposes that pharmacological enhancement of the therapeutic window can accelerate trauma processing. Even without MDMA, this suggests that creating conditions of reduced arousal and increased interpersonal safety may be more important than extended time. The question for long-term therapy is: what does the therapeutic relationship provide that MDMA provides pharmacologically?

## Secondary questions

### Continuous or interrupted?
Concentrated: 18 weeks total. Experimental sessions spaced 4 weeks apart with integration sessions between.

### One modality or many?
Combined: MDMA pharmacotherapy + manualized inner-directed psychotherapy. Two-therapist teams.

### One therapist or several?
Two therapists per participant (co-therapy team).

### Session frequency?
Variable: preparatory sessions not specified; experimental sessions every ~4 weeks; integration sessions between.

### Phase-based or organic?
Structured protocol: preparation → experimental → integration. Not the phase-based model of Herman/Cloitre, but has a clear phase structure.

### Therapy alone or therapy-plus?
Paradigmatic therapy-plus: pharmacological enhancement is the central innovation.

## Quality notes
Well-designed Phase 3 RCT published in Nature Medicine. However, the FDA rejection in 2024 casts significant doubt on the evidence. Key concerns: functional unblinding (many participants could tell if they received MDMA), therapist misconduct in earlier trials raising questions about the therapeutic frame, and short follow-up period. The 2024 corrected analysis of the Gorman PTG data (removing data from the therapist pair involved in ethical violations) is also relevant context. The trial was largely sponsored by MAPS (Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies), a nonprofit advocacy organization, raising questions about independence. Despite these concerns, the core efficacy signal (d=0.91) is strong and consistent with Phase 2 data.

## Cross-cutting observations

### Survivorship bias
Minimal for the RCT itself (ITT analysis). But exclusion criteria removed the most complex cases before randomization. The 345 assessed for eligibility were reduced to 91 randomized -- 73% were excluded, mostly for not meeting criteria. This is massive selection.

### Dose vs. elapsed time
3 MDMA sessions in 18 weeks = extremely compressed dose. The "dose" includes both the pharmacological (3 x 80-180mg MDMA) and the therapeutic (15 sessions total). The absence of long-term follow-up means we don't know if 18 weeks of elapsed time is sufficient for lasting change.

### Therapy vs. life
RCT design with placebo control partially addresses this. The supportive therapy provided to both groups produced substantial improvement in the placebo arm (32% lost PTSD diagnosis), suggesting that the therapy component alone is significant.

### Access and context
MDMA is not FDA-approved, Schedule I, and available only in research settings. This is a profound access barrier. Even if approved, the protocol requires two trained therapists for 8-hour sessions -- extremely resource-intensive and expensive. No mention of cost or insurance considerations.

### Definition of endpoint
CAPS-5 score change and loss of PTSD diagnosis. SDS for functional impairment. BDI-II for depression. All symptom-based. No measures of structural personality change, attachment, or identity.

### Dropout data
Low dropout: 42/46 MDMA completers (91%), 37/44 placebo completers (84%). One MDMA participant withdrew because of "feeling cured" after 2 sessions. Several COVID-related withdrawals.
