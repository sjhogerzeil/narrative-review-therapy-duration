## Cross-layer patterns emerging from this layer

1. **The wounded healer pattern**: 3 of 5 memoirists (Levine, Walker, Anderson) are clinicians who developed or champion therapeutic methods. Their healing is entangled with their professional development, making "therapy vs. life" inseparable.
2. **Endpoint convergence**: All 5 define healing relationally/functionally, not as symptom-free. Foo: capacity for connection. Levine: capacity to help others. Walker: thriving. Anderson: capacity to love/be loved. Eger: self-forgiveness.
3. **Duration convergence**: All 5 describe decade-plus trajectories. Range: 10+ years (Foo) to 72 years (Eger). No memoir describes rapid recovery.
4. **Modality-switching as norm**: Foo (most explicit), Walker, and Levine all describe movement through multiple approaches. None describes a single modality as sufficient.
5. **Access as invisible variable**: Only Foo directly addresses cost/access barriers ($6,400 pro bono therapy). The other four memoirists had significant professional/educational resources.
