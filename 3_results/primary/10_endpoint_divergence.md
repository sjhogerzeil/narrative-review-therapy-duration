# Endpoint divergence (emergent construct)

*Identified during Layer 1 annotation. The definition of "healed" determines the reported duration.*

## How endpoints determine duration

| Endpoint level | What changes | Typical timeline | Measured by | Sources |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| Symptom reduction | Fewer flashbacks, less hyperarousal, improved sleep | Weeks to months | PCL-5, BDI, HDRS | Howard 1986, EMDR literature |
| Functional improvement | Holding a job, maintaining relationships, reduced crises | Months to 1-2 years | GAF, work ability, SAS-SR | Links 2015 (GPM milestones), Knekt/Helsinki |
| Structural change | Personality reorganization, mentalization, integration of dissociated parts | Years | LPO, SASB, DSQ, DES, clinician assessment | Lindfors 2019, Bateman 2008, Steele 2005, Griffiths 2025 |
| Growth/reconnection | Agency, meaning, relational openness, "tolerable selfhood" | Years to ongoing | SWAP, PTG measures, qualitative | Herman 1992, Murphy 2009, Joseph 2012 |

## Why this matters

Much of the disagreement about therapy duration dissolves when you specify the endpoint:
- **Paris (2020)** argues brief therapy works — measuring symptom reduction and basic functional improvement
- **Steele (2005)** argues long therapy is needed — measuring structural integration of dissociated parts
- **Murphy (2009)** describes 160 sessions — measuring growth, meaning, quality of life

They are not disagreeing about the same thing. They are measuring different levels of change.

## Implication for the thesis

The thesis "healing takes years" is true at the structural/growth level and false at the symptom level. The review should explicitly decompose its duration claims by endpoint level rather than making a blanket claim.

## The endpoint question as a finding

The question "what counts as healed?" is not a methodological footnote — it is the central finding of this review. The answer to "how long does it take?" is: "it depends on what you mean by healing."

The table above makes the pattern unmistakable:
- **Symptom reduction** endpoints (CAPS-5, CORE-OM, diagnostic remission) produce durations of weeks to months
- **Functional improvement** endpoints (GAF, social functioning, vocational status) produce durations of months to 1-2 years
- **Structural change** endpoints (personality organization, mentalization, integration of dissociated parts) produce durations of years
- **Growth/reconnection** endpoints (PTG, identity transformation, relational capacity) produce durations of years to decades

The counterpoint sources (Layer 3) overwhelmingly use symptom reduction endpoints. The supporting sources (Layers 1, 4, 6) overwhelmingly use structural or growth endpoints. Much of the "disagreement" about duration dissolves when you specify what is being measured.
