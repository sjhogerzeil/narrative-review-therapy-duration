## The endpoint IS the answer

The most striking finding across the data: **the reported duration is almost entirely determined by the definition of the endpoint.** When healing is defined as symptom reduction (loss of diagnosis, score below clinical threshold), the evidence supports weeks to months. When healing is defined as structural change (personality integration, mentalizing capacity, earned security, relational capacity), the evidence supports years to decades.

The three endpoint levels map onto different evidence layers:

1. **Symptom reduction** — Layers 2-3 (naturalistic/counterpoint). Measurable by standardised instruments. Achievable in weeks (EMDR intensive) to months (standard therapy). This is what RCTs measure and what insurance funds.

2. **Functional improvement** — Layers 1a-2 (trauma-clinical/naturalistic). Measurable by vocational status, relationship quality, daily functioning. Emerges over months to years. Often continues growing after treatment ends (sleeper effect).

3. **Structural change** — Layers 1a-1d, 4, 6 (academic/practitioner/memoir). Defined variously as personality integration, mentalizing capacity, earned security, capacity for mutual recognition. Takes years to decades. May never reach a discrete endpoint — capacity continues deepening.

These are not competing definitions but nested levels. Symptom reduction is necessary but insufficient for functional improvement, which is necessary but insufficient for structural change. The duration question cannot be answered without specifying which level of change is meant.
