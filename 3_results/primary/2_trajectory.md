# Trajectory

*What does the path look like? Linear, nonlinear, spiral, episodic? What are common phases?*

## Trajectory descriptions by source

### Layer 1: Academic and clinical literature

**griffiths-2025-phase-oriented-review** — Nonlinear, phasic, recursive. Patients cycle through three phases (stabilisation, trauma processing, reintegration). Progress in later phases sometimes produces re-traumatisation as new dissociated material emerges. Appearance of regression when actually progressing to deeper processing. Symptoms improve at different rates: affect/impulse management (d=1.61) faster than dissociation itself (d=0.63).

**herman-1992-trauma-recovery** — Spiral rather than linear. Three phases (safety, remembrance and mourning, reconnection) are sequential in logic but recursive in practice. Patients cycle through them, returning to safety when processing or reconnection destabilises. Reconnection is lifelong and open-ended. The trajectory IS the recovery — it extends beyond any treatment endpoint.

**links-2015-gpm-bpd** — Linear, milestone-based. Distress reduction first (6 weeks), then behavioral stabilisation (6 months), then interpersonal improvement (12 months), then social/vocational functioning (18 months). Explicitly cumulative. Failure to meet milestones is diagnostic information about treatment fit.

**steele-2005-phase-oriented** — Explicitly nonlinear, spiral. Three phases framed as overcoming phobias (attachment, traumatic memories, normal life). "Periodic need to return to previous phases." Complexity of dissociation determines pace: more parts = more phobias = more recursive cycles. Phase-based in structure but organic in execution.

**vanderhart-2006-haunted-self** — Phase-based but recursive. Paradox at Phase 1: treatment cannot begin until patient tolerates the relationship, but tolerating the relationship IS the treatment. For tertiary dissociation (DID), the work must be done with each dissociative part, multiplying the trajectory.

**steele-2001-dependency** — Gradual relational development within the therapeutic dyad. From insecure dependency to secure dependency to autonomy. Not a separate phase but a continuous thread running through all phases. Complicated by contradictory attachment needs of different dissociative parts.

**kohut-1984-how-analysis-cures** — Developmental rather than linear. Initial idealisation of analyst as selfobject, followed by thousands of cycles of attunement, optimal frustration, and repair. Gradual transmuting internalization building micro-increments of psychic structure. Trajectory of diminishing need for external regulation. "Borrowed" vs "owned" structure distinguishes premature from genuine change.

**winnicott-1965-maturational** — Developmentally phased but organic. Establishing holding environment, deepening regression, resuming maturational process, gradual independence. Patient gets "worse" before "better" — regression is the treatment, not a complication. False self must be dismantled before true self can emerge.

**bromberg-1998-standing-spaces** — Gradual expansion. Cyclical enactment-recognition-repair cycles. Each cycle slightly expands the territory of self-states the patient can hold in awareness simultaneously. No prescribed sequence — which self-states become accessible is determined by the unfolding relational process.

**benjamin-1988-bonds-of-love** — Cyclical: doer/done-to -> enactment -> survival -> thirdness -> collapse -> repeat. Each cycle builds slightly more capacity for sustained mutual recognition. Thirdness is never permanently achieved — it is created, lost, and re-found.

**fonagy-2002-affect-regulation** — Developmental trajectory from pre-mentalizing modes (psychic equivalence, pretend mode, teleological mode) toward genuine mentalizing. External regulation (therapist provides mentalizing) gradually gives way to internalized capacity.

**hauser-2022-fragile-process** — "A slow, circular movement." First 15 sessions spent "becoming aware." Oscillation between extreme high intensity and extreme low intensity (fragile process). Circular rather than linear: "pulling off a little more of that dressing" each session. Organic, not phase-based.

**joseph-2012-ptg-model** — Repetitive cyclic process: event cognitions -> appraisal -> emotional states -> coping -> reappraisal. Continues until assumptive world accommodates new trauma-related information. Shift from ruminative brooding to reflective pondering is key. Curvilinear: too much stress impedes growth.

**murphy-2009-pct-abuse** — Nonlinear with discernible emergent phases. Sessions 1-26: disclosure, trust-building. Session 26: first social venture ("liked by people"). Session 68: full accurate symbolisation of core trauma (turning point). Session 93: reconnected with son after 17 years. Growth events occurred at uneven intervals. "Old ways of being returned" periodically.

**murphy-2019-pct-eft-trauma** — Only early trajectory examined (first 3 sessions). Good outcomes: alliance formation -> trauma recognition -> connecting past to present. Poor outcomes: these steps were absent. The early trajectory determines whether deeper work can follow.

**bcpsg-2010-change-psychotherapy** — Fundamentally incremental and unpredictable. Small shifts interspersed with stasis. Not a smooth curve and not a dramatic breakthrough — gradual accretion of relational experience through "moments of meeting." Tipping point cannot be predicted.

**porges-2022-polyvagal** — From chronic defensive autonomic states toward ventral vagal dominance. Not linear: dissolution hierarchy means stress triggers regression to older defensive patterns even after partial reorganisation. Window of social engagement gradually expands from most controlled therapeutic environment to wider contexts.

**hambrick-perry-2019-nmt** — Sequential, bottom-up. Brainstem regulation -> limbic relational engagement -> cortical cognitive processing. Each layer must be addressed before the next can begin. Not just one trajectory but multiple stacked trajectories.

**schore-2000-right-brain / schore-2021-intersubjectivity** — Accumulative. Repeated cycles of attunement, disruption, and repair gradually reshape orbitofrontal regulatory structures. Moment-to-moment right-brain synchronization aggregates into structural change over time.

**lanius-2011-neurobiology-cptsd** — Multi-system, sequential. Emotion regulation first, then self-referential processing, then social cognition, then consciousness/awareness. Two subtypes (undermodulation vs overmodulation) require different starting points.

### Layer 2: Naturalistic outcome studies

**howard-1986-dose-effect** — Negatively accelerated (log-linear) curve. Steep early gains, progressively flattening. Each additional session contributes less than the previous. Curve shifts rightward for more severe pathology.

**fonagy-2015-tads** — Sleeper effect trajectory. During 18 months of treatment: improvement but not significantly more than TAU. After treatment ended: LTPP group continued improving while TAU deteriorated. By 42 months: dramatic divergence. Opposite of the typical dose-response curve.

**bateman-fonagy-2008-mbt-8yr** — Sleeper effect. Gains did not plateau or diminish after treatment — they continued growing over 5-year post-treatment period. TAU deteriorated despite receiving substantially MORE services. Vocational functioning gradually increased across three post-discharge periods.

**shedler-2010-efficacy** — Catalytic trajectory. Psychodynamic therapy sets in motion processes that continue autonomously after treatment. Effect sizes INCREASE at follow-up — unique to psychodynamic approach. Not dose-response but capacity-installation.

**lindfors-2019-helsinki-10yr** — Convergence trajectory. LPP patients improved faster (shorter treatment); PA patients improved later (post-treatment acceleration). By year 10: convergence. Different routes to similar endpoint.

**zimmermann-2014-munich-dose** — Dual-track trajectory. Dose mediates interpersonal outcomes at post-treatment. Technique mediates symptom outcomes at follow-up. Different mechanisms dominate at different timepoints.

### Layer 3: Counterpoint

**dejongh-2024-emdr-science / dejongh-2023-emdr-case** — Rapid, targeted memory reprocessing. Linear and goal-directed: identify memories, process them, verify resolution. No stabilisation phase, no extended relationship-building period.

**mitchell-2021-mdma-phase3** — Concentrated, pharmacologically enhanced. Three 8-hour sessions as core, with preparation and integration. Change is rapid during 18-week protocol. MDMA creates a "window of tolerance" for rapid processing.

**feder-2025-ketamine-exposure** — Rapid, pharmacologically primed. Ketamine enhances neuroplasticity, then brief exposure therapy follows. Response rapid (Week 3) and durable in 61.5% at 6 months.

**sciarrino-2020-intensive-review** — Compressed standard treatment. Same ESTs delivered at higher frequency (daily instead of weekly). Maintains or exceeds effect sizes while dramatically reducing dropout. Format change, not process change.

**farina-2025-rapid-rare** — Two distinct trajectories for CPTSD: rapid (for patients with sufficient adaptive information processing capacity) and extended (for deeper structural disruption). Same diagnosis, different underlying structures, different trajectories.

**paris-2020-access-pd** — Naturalistic recovery trajectory. BPD improves gradually over 24 years with decreasing relapse. Much of this is natural course, not therapy. Therapy adds early stabilisation, not long-term accompaniment.

### Layer 4: Practitioner essays

**substack-traumalescence** — Developmental transition with explicit timeline: 3-6 months (awareness), 6-18 months (neural strengthening), 18 months-3 years (integration). Apparent regression during strengthening phase is actually progress. Analogous to adolescence.

**psychnetworker-evolving-beyond** — Two-phase trajectory: backward-looking (grief, processing) -> forward-looking (identity creation, future-orientation). The transition point is when the client recognises they are perpetuating old patterns.

**psychnetworker-transcending-trauma** — Long-arc, nonlinear. Setbacks expected. Growth emerges over years, not months. Symptoms reframed as "lifesaving emergency measures" that are gradually released.

**shedler-2020-tyranny-of-time** — Dose-dependent improvement with no ceiling at 2+ years. With traumatised clients, "the real story only comes out after about a year."

**blueknot-trauma-practice** — Three phases (stabilisation, processing, consolidation) that are NOT linear. Safety must be re-established repeatedly. May proceed "continuously or episodically" — over decades or a lifetime.

**grossmark-psychoanalytic-companioning** — Extended periods of just being accompanied before any processing can occur. Companioning is not a technique but a relational posture — inherently open-ended.

### Layer 5: Client narratives

**myptsd-2019-how-long-therapy** — Escalation gap pattern: years of unhelpful therapy due to poor relational fit, followed by rapid progress once the right therapist is found.

**myptsd-2021-how-long-therapy-2** — Typology of engagement patterns: continuous long-term, episodic/cyclical, quality-driven (3 months of good therapy > years of poor therapy), and ongoing-by-choice (therapy as practice, not cure).

**myptsd-2024-emdr-cptsd-duration** — Even with EMDR, CPTSD clients report years (2-5+), not the 8-16 sessions from RCTs. RCT-reality gap is enormous.

### Layer 6: Memoirs

**foo_2022_what_my_bones_know** — 8 years before accurate diagnosis. Then modality-switching. Then finding the right therapist (Dr. Ham). Multi-decade total arc.

**walker_2013_complex_ptsd** — "Somewhat linear progression" while acknowledging nonlinearity. 40+ year journey. Four F responses as conceptual framework.

**eger_2017_the_choice** — 35 years of suppression before processing began. Physical return to Auschwitz as turning point. Healing described as ongoing at 90+. Four-stage structure: Prison -> Escape -> Freedom -> Healing.

## Emergent trajectory typology

From cross-layer analysis, five trajectory shapes emerge:

1. **Dose-response curve** (Howard 1986, Paris 2020): Negatively accelerated — rapid early gains, diminishing returns. Best fits mild-moderate presentations.

2. **Sleeper/catalytic effect** (Shedler 2010, Fonagy 2015, Bateman & Fonagy 2008): Gains grow AFTER treatment ends. Therapy installs capacity; life exercises it.

3. **Spiral/recursive** (Herman 1992, Steele 2005, Van der Hart 2006, Bromberg 1998): Cycling through phases, returning to earlier work as deeper material emerges. Phase-based in structure, organic in execution.

4. **Episodic/cyclical** (Blue Knot, myptsd-2021, client narratives): Periods of intensive engagement alternating with self-managed maintenance. "Continuously or episodically" over years or decades.

5. **Rapid/targeted** (EMDR, MDMA, intensive formats): Concentrated symptom reduction. Linear, goal-directed. Best fits single-system problems or patients with high baseline capacity.
