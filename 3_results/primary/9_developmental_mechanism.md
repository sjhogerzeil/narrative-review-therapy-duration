# Developmental mechanism convergence (emergent construct)

*Identified during Layer 1 annotation. Five independent traditions describe the same underlying process.*

## The convergence

| Tradition | Author | Mechanism | Unit of change |
|-----------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Self psychology | Kohut (1984) | Transmuting internalization | Optimal frustration → micro-internalization cycles |
| Object relations | Winnicott (1965) | Regression to dependence → resumed maturation | Holding environment sustained over developmental time |
| Relational psychoanalysis | Bromberg (1998) | Self-state expansion | Capacity to "stand in the spaces" between states |
| Intersubjectivity | Benjamin (1988) | Mutual recognition | Repeated destruction-survival cycles |
| Mentalization | Fonagy et al. (2002) | Marked mirroring → mentalizing capacity | Attachment-based experiential learning |
| Affect regulation neuroscience | Schore (2000, 2021) | Right-brain regulatory structure | Experience-dependent neuroplasticity |
| Polyvagal theory | Porges (2022) | Autonomic reorganization | Repeated co-regulation → neuroception shift |
| Neurosequential model | Perry (2019) | Bottom-up developmental layers | Patterned, repetitive, sequential experience |
| Implicit process | BCPSG (2010) | Implicit relational knowing | "Accumulation of many small shifts" |

## Why this matters for duration

All nine descriptions share three features:
1. **Repeated relational cycles** — change happens through many iterations, not insight
2. **Gradual structural change** — what changes is internal structure (not symptoms)
3. **Inherently temporal** — the process cannot be compressed by technique or frequency

This means duration is not a preference or a resource question — it is a structural requirement of the change process itself. The biology sets the pace.

## Key distinction from dose-effect

Howard's dose-effect model (Layer 2) measures sessions. These developmental mechanisms suggest the relevant "dose" is not sessions but **completed relational cycles** — attunement-disruption-repair sequences that may or may not occur in any given session. This reframes the dose question fundamentally.

## Implications for the thesis

The convergence across independent traditions is the review's strongest theoretical argument: multiple traditions, using different concepts and methods, arrive at the same conclusion about why deep change takes years. The probability that this convergence is coincidental is low.
