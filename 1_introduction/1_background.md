# Background

*To be written. This section sets up why the question of duration matters.*

## Key points to develop

- Complex developmental trauma is distinct from single-incident PTSD — chronic, relational, early-onset, personality-organizing
- The dominant clinical culture underestimates the time required for deep change in this population
- Insurance structures, public expectations, and evidence-based practice discourse all exert pressure toward brevity
- There is a gap between what clinicians who work with this population know experientially and what the literature formally states
- The question "how long does it take?" is not merely practical — it reflects deeper assumptions about what healing is, what therapy does, and what change means at the level of self-organization
