# The definition of healing

The definition of healing is itself part of the problem. Different definitions produce different duration estimates — and much of the confusion in the literature stems from conflating fundamentally different endpoints.

## Working definition

*The capacity to live with agency, relational openness, and tolerable selfhood, rather than organized around survival.*

This is acknowledged as contested. Different traditions define the endpoint differently, and this review treats "what counts as healed?" as a finding, not a premise.

## Levels of change (not a hierarchy — a taxonomy)

- **Symptom reduction** — fewer flashbacks, less hyperarousal, improved sleep. Measurable in weeks to months. The primary outcome in most RCTs.
- **Functional improvement** — holding a job, maintaining relationships, reduced crisis episodes. Measurable in months to years. Often the endpoint for insurance and service delivery.
- **Structural change** — reorganization of self, relational patterns, defensive structures, epistemic trust. The person is not merely managing symptoms but has become someone who processes experience differently. Measurable in years — if measurable at all.

## Why this matters for the duration question

If "healed" means symptom reduction, many interventions work in 12–20 sessions. If "healed" means structural change, the timeline extends to years. The literature's reported durations are only interpretable once the definition of the endpoint is made explicit.
