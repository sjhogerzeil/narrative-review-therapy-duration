# Research questions

## Primary question

What does the clinical literature, practitioner wisdom, lived experience, and autobiographical record tell us about how long healing from complex developmental trauma takes — what the trajectory looks like, what determines the timeline, and whether the question as commonly posed contains assumptions that explain the field's inability to agree on an answer?

## Secondary questions — how does healing unfold structurally?

The primary question concerns duration. The secondary questions concern the structure of the process — how healing unfolds in practice, across the dimensions that shape the trajectory. These are collected per source alongside the primary question. No data is a valid answer — the pattern of what sources fail to address is itself a finding.

1. **Continuous or interrupted?** — Is uninterrupted therapy necessary, or do breaks, pauses, and resumptions serve the process? What role do forced interruptions (financial, life events, therapist departure) play?
2. **One modality or many?** — Does healing happen within a single therapeutic framework, or do people typically move through multiple modalities — sequentially, in parallel, or layered? Is modality-switching a sign of progress or of failure?
3. **One therapist or several?** — Is the long-term relationship with one therapist essential, or do different therapists serve different developmental needs at different stages?
4. **Session frequency?** — Once a week, twice, three times? Does higher frequency shorten calendar duration, or is elapsed life-time itself part of the medicine?
5. **Phase-based or organic?** — Does healing follow a predictable phase structure (stabilisation → processing → integration), or is the trajectory more emergent and nonlinear?
6. **Therapy alone or therapy-plus?** — What role do adjuncts play — medication, bodywork, group therapy, peer support, creative practice, spiritual community — and are they sequential or concurrent with the primary therapy?
