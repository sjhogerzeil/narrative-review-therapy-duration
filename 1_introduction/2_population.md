# Clinical population

People with severe developmental trauma — chronic, relational, often beginning in early childhood — who have organized their personality, relational patterns, defensive structures, and sense of self around survival. Characterized by significant dissociative features, collapsed agency, core shame, and fragmented self-organization. Often meet criteria for complex PTSD or personality disorders (understood as traumatic adaptation, not primary pathology).

## Distinguishing features

- Trauma is not a single event but a developmental environment
- The "disorder" is not a symptom overlay but a structural adaptation — personality, relational patterns, and self-organization are shaped by survival
- Dissociation is structural, not episodic
- Core shame and collapsed agency are central, not peripheral
- The required work is not symptom reduction but a fundamental restructuring of epistemic trust, relational capacity, subjectivity, and preperceptive self-organization

## Diagnostic frameworks

- ICD-11 complex PTSD (distinct from PTSD)
- DSM personality disorders (reframed as traumatic adaptation)
- Structural dissociation model (van der Hart, Nijenhuis, Steele)
- Developmental trauma disorder (van der Kolk, proposed but not adopted)
