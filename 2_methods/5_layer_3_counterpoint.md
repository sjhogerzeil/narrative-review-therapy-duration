# Layer 3: Counterpoint — accelerated and brief models

The strongest claims that deep change can happen faster. Not to dismiss, but to engage honestly. What do these models actually claim? For which population? What do they mean by "change"?

## Models to examine

- **AEDP** (Diana Fosha) — accelerated experiential dynamic psychotherapy; claims rapid access to core affect and transformation
- **ISTDP** (Habib Davanloo / Jon Frederickson) — intensive short-term dynamic psychotherapy; claims breakthrough in complex cases
- **EMDR** — not as brief protocol, but claims of structural change beyond symptom reduction
- **Ketamine/MDMA-assisted therapy** — claims of accelerated trauma processing
- **Intensive outpatient/retreat models** — concentrated formats (multiple sessions/week, multi-day intensives)

## Annotation framework for each model

- What population are they actually studying? (Often not the population defined in this review)
- What do they define as the outcome? (Symptom reduction vs. structural change)
- What is the evidence base? (RCTs, case studies, clinical claims)
- What is the strongest version of their argument?
- Under what conditions could this model be right — and what would that mean for the working thesis?
- Where are the genuine limits of applicability for the population in this review?
- What can the long-duration position learn from this model, even if the model doesn't fully apply?
