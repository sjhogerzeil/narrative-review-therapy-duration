# Search strategy

## Inclusion principle

Material that addresses duration, trajectory, or temporal experience of healing from complex developmental trauma — from any tradition, methodology, or epistemological stance.

## Exclusion principle

Material focused exclusively on single-incident PTSD, acute stabilization without long-term follow-up, or populations without developmental trauma. Brief protocol studies are excluded from the main review but included in Layer 3 (counterpoint) when they make claims about this population.

**Softened exclusion:** Phase-based frameworks (e.g., ISSTD guidelines) are included even if they emphasize stabilization — because their treatment duration estimates are directly relevant. RCTs with longer follow-up periods are included if they capture meaningful duration data.

## Iterative search protocol (applies to every layer)

Each layer follows a search → annotate → extend cycle:

1. **Search** using the planned terms, sources, and authors listed in the layer-specific methods files (3–8).
2. **Annotate** each relevant source using `3_results/sources/SOURCE_TEMPLATE.md`.
3. **Extend** — while annotating, watch for:
   - Authors, papers, or books cited in the source that were not in the original plan
   - New search terms suggested by the language or framing of the source
   - Adjacent traditions, debates, or populations that bear on the question
   - Sources that belong in a *different* layer than where they were found (e.g., a practitioner essay discovered via PubMed → move to Layer 4)
4. **Log the extension** in the layer's `README.md` under `## Discovered sources`: what was found, where it was found, and why it's relevant.
5. **Update the layer-specific methods file** if the discovery changes the search terms, key authors, or scope. Mark additions with `[added YYYY-MM-DD]` so evolution is traceable.

The goal is a snowball process: each source annotated should surface at least one lead that wasn't planned.

## Stopping rule

Each layer has a source budget. After reaching the budget, only add a new source if it offers something not already captured (a new duration estimate, a different population segment, a genuinely distinct theoretical argument). Log the decision to stop in the layer's README.

| Layer | Initial budget | Max with extensions |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1a. Trauma-clinical | 10 | 15 |
| 1b. Psychoanalytic | 5 | 8 |
| 1c. Humanistic-existential | 5 | 8 |
| 1d. Neuroscience | 5 | 8 |
| 2. Naturalistic studies | 8 | 12 |
| 3. Counterpoint | 5 per model (25 total) | 8 per model |
| 4. Practitioner | 8 | 12 |
| 5. Client narratives | 10 | 15 |
| 6. Memoirs | 5 | 8 |
